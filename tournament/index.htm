🏟️ Tournament Bracket

From a list of names to a crowned champion

 

Click a name to advance them · 🎲 decides a match at random

Tournament Bracket Generator

Office ping-pong championship, FIFA night, spelling bee, best-meme contest — everything is more official with a bracket. Paste your participants, hit create, and get a clean single-elimination bracket with random seeding. Play real matches and click the winners, or let the dice decide everything and crown a champion in thirty seconds — confetti included.

How to use it

  1. Paste participants — 2 to 64 names, one per line.
  2. Create the bracket — names are shuffled and seeded randomly; byes are added automatically when the count isn't a power of two.
  3. Decide matches — click the winner of each real game, or hit 🎲 on any match for a random result.
  4. Crown the champion — the final winner gets the banner and the confetti. Copy the full results to share.

Byes, seeding and fairness

Single elimination needs a power-of-two field: 4, 8, 16, 32 or 64. With, say, 11 players, the generator rounds up to 16 and hands out 5 byes — free passes through round one. Byes are assigned by the same random shuffle as the matchups, so no one can accuse the organizer of favoritism. Every bracket re-creation is a fresh Fisher–Yates shuffle, the same unbiased algorithm behind our Team Generator and Raffle Picker.

Ways to run it

  • Real tournaments: project the bracket, play the games, click winners as they happen. Changed a result by mistake? Click the other name — downstream matches reset automatically.
  • Pure luck mode: "Randomize remaining" simulates the whole tournament instantly — perfect for raffles with knockout drama instead of a simple draw.
  • Hybrid: decide the early rounds by dice, play the finals for real. Maximum stakes, minimum scheduling.

FAQ

Does it support double elimination or round robin?
Not yet — single elimination covers most casual tournaments. For round-robin-style repeated random picks, the wheel spinner with winner removal works great.

Are the matchups really random?
Yes — a Fisher–Yates shuffle seeds the bracket, so all pairings are equally likely. Re-create for a new draw.

Can I save the bracket?
Copy the results text anytime. The bracket also stays on screen until you leave or reset the page.
